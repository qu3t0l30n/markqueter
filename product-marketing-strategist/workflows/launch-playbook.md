<process>

**Phase 1: Launch readiness assessment**

Before planning tactics, assess readiness across four dimensions (Cagan's Four Risks applied to launch):

**Value readiness:**
- Has the value proposition been validated with real users? (not just internal feedback)
- Do you have at least 3 concrete proof points (metrics, quotes, case studies)?
- Can you clearly articulate the "before and after" transformation?
- Score: Ready / Partially Ready / Not Ready

**Market readiness:**
- Is the target audience defined specifically enough to reach them?
- Do you know where they spend time, who they listen to, and what triggers their buying process?
- Have you tested messaging with this audience?
- Score: Ready / Partially Ready / Not Ready

**Product readiness:**
- Is the core experience reliable and instrumented for measurement?
- Does onboarding deliver the "aha moment" within the first session?
- Are known bugs or UX issues going to undermine the launch narrative?
- Score: Ready / Partially Ready / Not Ready

**Operational readiness:**
- Can sales demo the product confidently?
- Is customer support prepared for increased volume?
- Is the measurement infrastructure in place (analytics, attribution, alerting)?
- Score: Ready / Partially Ready / Not Ready

If any dimension is "Not Ready" — address that gap before launching. A premature launch burns the most valuable resource: first impressions.

**Phase 2: Launch tier classification**

Not every launch deserves the same investment. Classify using this framework:

| Tier | Description | Effort | Examples |
|------|-------------|--------|---------|
| **Tier 1 — Flagship** | New product, new market, or transformative capability | Full campaign: press, email, social, events, sales enablement | New product line, major platform release, category entry |
| **Tier 2 — Major** | Significant new capability for existing product | Targeted campaign: blog, email, in-app, sales update | Major feature, new integration, pricing change |
| **Tier 3 — Standard** | Important improvement, new feature | Lightweight: changelog, in-app notification, email mention | Feature improvements, new workflows, UI updates |
| **Tier 4 — Continuous** | Ongoing improvements | Minimal: product changelog, release notes | Bug fixes, performance improvements, minor updates |

Ask the user: "Based on this framework, what tier is your launch?" If they say Tier 1 for something that is clearly Tier 3, challenge them. Over-launching erodes audience trust.

**Phase 3: Pre-launch (weeks -4 to -1)**

**Week -4: Foundation**
- Finalize positioning (use positioning-strategy workflow if not done)
- Complete product narrative (use product-narrative workflow if not done)
- Define launch metrics and success criteria (avoid vanity metrics — use outcome metrics)
- Build the measurement plan: what will you track, how, and when

**Week -3: Content and assets**
- Create launch assets appropriate to the tier:
  - Tier 1: Landing page, blog post, press release, demo video, sales deck, email sequences, social content
  - Tier 2: Blog post, email announcement, in-app messaging, sales one-pager
  - Tier 3: Changelog entry, in-app notification, email mention
- Build sales enablement materials (battlecards, FAQ, objection handling)
- Prepare customer reference stories if available

**Week -2: Internal alignment**
- Brief all customer-facing teams (sales, CS, support)
- Run a "launch rehearsal" — walk through the customer journey from first touch to activation
- Identify gaps in the experience and fix critical ones
- Set up monitoring dashboards for launch metrics

**Week -1: Seeding**
- Seed content with early access customers, analysts, or community members
- Begin drip campaign to warm the audience
- Final QA on all assets, links, tracking, and product experience
- Prepare contingency plan (what to do if something breaks on launch day)

**Phase 4: Launch day execution**

**Launch day checklist:**
- [ ] Publish all content assets (stagger across the day for sustained visibility)
- [ ] Send email announcements (segment by relevance)
- [ ] Activate in-app messaging
- [ ] Brief sales on live deal implications
- [ ] Monitor real-time metrics: traffic, signups, activation, errors
- [ ] Monitor social and community channels for feedback
- [ ] Capture feedback and early user reactions (these are gold for iteration)

**Launch day mindset (Perri + Cagan):**
- This is not a celebration — it is the start of an experiment
- Every piece of data you collect today informs what you do tomorrow
- Be ready to adjust messaging, fix UX issues, and respond to unexpected reactions

**Phase 5: Post-launch learning (days 1-90)**

This is where most product marketing teams fail. The plan for after launch is more important than the launch itself.

**Week 1 (Days 1-7):**
- Analyze: Who signed up? Do they match the target persona?
- Measure: What is the Day-1 and Day-7 activation rate?
- Listen: What are users saying in support tickets, social media, and reviews?
- Act: Fix any critical onboarding issues immediately
- Report: Share early results with the team (facts, not spin)

**Week 2-4 (Days 8-30):**
- Analyze activation funnel: Where are new users dropping off?
- Run message testing: Is the launch positioning resonating or falling flat?
- Iterate: Adjust landing page, onboarding, or email based on data
- Begin building case studies from early adopters
- Assess: Is the growth loop working? If not, why?

**Month 2-3 (Days 31-90):**
- Measure retention cohorts: Are launch users retaining better or worse than pre-launch?
- Conduct win/loss interviews with prospects who evaluated and chose (or did not choose) the product
- Refine positioning based on actual market feedback
- Scale what is working, kill what is not
- Update the product narrative based on real customer stories

**Phase 6: Launch retrospective**

At Day 90, conduct a structured retrospective:

1. **Metrics review**: Did we hit our launch success criteria? Why or why not?
2. **Positioning validation**: Was our positioning confirmed, adjusted, or invalidated by the market?
3. **Growth loop assessment**: Is the loop working? What is the loop efficiency?
4. **Customer feedback synthesis**: What did we learn about our customers that we did not know before?
5. **Process improvement**: What would we do differently next time?
6. **Next actions**: Based on everything learned, what should product marketing focus on for the next 90 days?

</process>

<success_criteria>
- Launch readiness is assessed across all four dimensions before proceeding
- Launch tier is correctly classified (not over-inflated)
- Pre-launch plan includes positioning, narrative, and measurement
- Post-launch plan allocates 70% of effort to the 90 days after launch
- Metrics are outcome-based (adoption, retention, revenue) not vanity (impressions, clicks)
- Learning plan is specific: what to measure, when, and what decisions it informs
- Anti-patterns are avoided: no Launch and Forget, no Feature Factory Marketing Machine
</success_criteria>
